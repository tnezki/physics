1. [DOK 1] Which statement about magnetic poles is correct?
A. Like poles attract.
B. Unlike poles repel.
C. Like poles repel and unlike poles attract.
D. Magnets contain only north poles.
E. Magnets contain only south poles.
2. [DOK 1] Magnetic field lines show:
A. electric current only
B. field direction and relative strength
C. physical strings leaving a magnet
D. energy stored inside metal
E. electric charge flow
3. [DOK 1] A material becomes strongly magnetic when many magnetic ______ align.
A. currents
B. domains
C. resistors
D. transformers
E. voltages
4. [DOK 1] An electromagnet becomes magnetic because:
A. it contains permanent electric charge
B. current flows through a coil of wire
C. voltage disappears inside the wire
D. field lines are destroyed
E. resistance becomes zero
5. [DOK 1] A generator primarily converts:
A. electrical energy into thermal energy
B. magnetic energy into charge
C. mechanical energy into electrical energy
D. current into resistance
E. voltage into poles
6. [DOK 2] A moving charge of 2 C travels at 3 m/s perpendicular to a 4 T magnetic field. What force acts on the charge?
A. 6 N
B. 12 N
C. 24 N
D. 48 N
E. 96 N
7. [DOK 2] A wire carrying 5 A is perpendicular to a magnetic field of 0.40 T over 0.20 m. What magnetic force acts on the wire?
A. 0.04 N
B. 0.40 N
C. 2.0 N
D. 4.0 N
E. 20 N
8. [DOK 2] A transformer has 100 turns on the primary and 500 turns on the secondary. If primary voltage is 12 V, what is secondary voltage?
A. 2.4 V
B. 12 V
C. 24 V
D. 60 V
E. 120 V
9. [DOK 2] A generator graph completes 4 cycles in 2 seconds. What is the frequency?
A. 0.5 Hz
B. 1 Hz
C. 2 Hz
D. 4 Hz
E. 8 Hz
10. [DOK 2] An ideal transformer has input power 60 W. Approximate output power?
A. 0 W
B. 30 W
C. 60 W
D. 120 W
E. cannot estimate
11. [DOK 2] Moving a magnet quickly into a coil compared to slowly into the same coil produces:
A. less induced voltage
B. greater induced voltage
C. identical induced voltage
D. zero induced voltage
E. current only if moving out
12. [DOK 3] A student says: 'If you cut a magnet in half, one piece becomes only north and the other only south.' Which response best corrects this?
A. magnets contain only electric charge
B. each piece becomes smaller magnet with both poles
C. one pole disappears
D. magnets stop working
E. domains vanish
13. [DOK 3] A compass near a wire deflects only when current flows. What does this show?
A. magnets create charge
B. moving charges create magnetic fields
C. voltage flows out
D. current destroys fields
E. compasses measure resistance
14. [DOK 3] A student says: 'A generator creates electrical energy from nothing.' Which response is best?
A. correct because fields generate energy
B. incorrect because generators convert mechanical energy into electrical energy
C. correct because induction creates free power
D. incorrect because generators destroy energy
E. correct only with AC
15. [DOK 3] Why can adding an iron core strengthen an electromagnet?
A. iron eliminates resistance
B. domains in iron align and strengthen field
C. iron creates charge
D. iron stops induction
E. field lines disappear
16. [DOK 4] A company claims a flashlight runs forever because it contains powerful permanent magnets. Best evaluation?
A. correct because magnets provide unlimited energy
B. correct because fields create current forever
C. incorrect because continued electrical output requires energy input or changing magnetic conditions
D. incorrect because permanent magnets contain no fields
E. correct only with AC

19. A junkyard crane uses an electromagnet to lift scrap metal. Explain why current is required and why turning off the current releases the metal.
20. A student designs a generator by placing a coil next to a magnet but keeping both still. Critique the design using induction and changing magnetic conditions.
