E. 566 m

A. $+1\,\text{cm}$
B. $+5\,\text{cm}$
C. $-5\,\text{cm}$
D. $-1\,\text{cm}$
E. $0\,\text{cm}$
12. [DOK 3] A student says, “A louder sound must have a higher pitch.” Which response best corrects the misconception?
A. Correct, because loudness and pitch are the same.
B. Incorrect, because loudness depends mainly on amplitude and pitch depends on frequency.
C. Correct, because amplitude controls pitch.
D. Incorrect, because pitch depends only on wavelength and never frequency.
E. Correct only for sounds in air.
13. [DOK 3] An ambulance siren sounds higher-pitched as it approaches and lower-pitched after it passes. Which explanation is best?
A. The siren changes its actual frequency every time it moves.
B. The air stops carrying the sound behind the ambulance.
C. Wavefronts are compressed in front and spread out behind, changing observed frequency.
D. Sound travels faster in front of the ambulance than behind it.
E. The amplitude becomes zero after the ambulance passes.
14. [DOK 3] A student says, “Sound travels because air particles move all the way from the speaker to your ear.” Which correction is best?
A. Air particles vibrate back and forth while energy travels through the medium.
B. Air particles do not move at all during sound.
C. Sound is carried only by empty space.
D. The speaker throws air particles directly into your ear.
E. Sound only travels as transverse waves.
15. [DOK 3] Noise-canceling headphones reduce sound mostly by:
A. creating a wave that always increases the original sound
B. using destructive interference with a matching opposite wave
C. changing sound into light
D. making sound travel without a medium
E. increasing the frequency of every sound
16. [DOK 4] A student claims a room can be made completely silent by playing one constant tone. Which evaluation is strongest?
A. Correct, because one tone cancels every possible sound.
B. Correct, because destructive interference never depends on frequency or timing.
C. Incorrect, because real noise contains many frequencies and changing wave patterns that require matched timing and frequency.
D. Incorrect, because sound cannot interfere with other sound.
E. Correct only if the room is very large.
