E. dry wood

D. total amount of charge
E. current stored in a battery

A. only a battery

B. open

D. short circuit
E. grounded circuit

7. [DOK 2] A resistor has 12 V across it and current 3 A through it. What is the resistance?
A. 0.25 Ω
B. 3 Ω
C. 4 Ω
D. 9 Ω
E. 36 Ω
8. [DOK 2] A device uses 24 J of energy for every 2 C of charge. What is the voltage?
A. 2 V
B. 6 V
C. 12 V
D. 24 V
E. 48 V
9. [DOK 2] A device has 120 V across it and draws 2 A. What is the power?
A. 60 W
B. 120 W
C. 240 W
D. 480 W
E. 2400 W
10. [DOK 2] Two resistors, 4 Ω and 6 Ω, are connected in series. What is the equivalent resistance?

D. 10 Ω
E. 24 Ω
11. [DOK 2] Two resistors, 6 Ω and 3 Ω, are connected in parallel. What is the equivalent resistance?
A. 1 Ω
B. 2 Ω
C. 3 Ω
D. 9 Ω
E. 18 Ω
12. [DOK 3] A student says: 'Current gets used up as it passes through each bulb.' Which response best corrects the misconception?
A. Current disappears after the first bulb.
B. Charge is conserved throughout the circuit.
C. Voltage and current are identical.
D. Bulbs absorb all electrons.
E. Current only exists near the battery.
13. [DOK 3] A student says: 'Voltage flows through the circuit.' Which response is best?
A. Correct because voltage is moving charge.
B. Incorrect because voltage is electric potential difference, not flowing matter.
C. Correct because batteries push voltage particles.
D. Incorrect because voltage only exists in open circuits.
E. Correct only in series circuits.
14. [DOK 3] Why are household circuits usually wired in parallel?
A. current can only exist in parallel circuits
B. each device can operate independently
C. voltage becomes zero in series circuits
D. resistance disappears in parallel circuits
E. batteries only work in parallel circuits
15. [DOK 3] A student says: 'A thicker wire should have more resistance because it contains more material.' Which response is best?
A. Correct because all larger objects have greater resistance.
B. Incorrect because thicker wires provide more paths for charge flow and usually reduce resistance.
C. Correct because thicker wires always reduce current.
D. Incorrect because resistance does not depend on wire size.
E. Correct only for insulators.
16. [DOK 4] A website claims a device lowers electric bills by 'recycling unused current.' Which evaluation is strongest?
A. Correct because unused current collects in outlets.
B. Correct because current can be permanently stored in wires.
C. Incorrect because current is charge flow, and bills depend on energy transfer and power use, not 'unused current.'
D. Incorrect because current and voltage are identical.
E. Correct only in series circuits.
