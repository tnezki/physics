Directions: Answer questions 1–16 on the answer sheet. Complete questions 17–20 on the back of the answer sheet. You may write on this assessment copy, but only the answer sheet gets graded. Test copies will be recycled after grading.
1. [DOK 1] Light is best described as:
A. a mechanical longitudinal wave
B. an electromagnetic wave
C. a wave that requires air
D. a type of matter
E. a vibration of gravity
2. [DOK 1] Which material allows light to pass through clearly?
A. opaque
B. reflective
C. transparent
D. diffuse
E. absorbent
3. [DOK 1] Reflection is:
A. bending of light
B. absorption of light
C. bouncing of light from a surface
D. splitting light into colors
E. slowing of light
4. [DOK 1] The line drawn perpendicular to a surface is called:
A. wavelength
B. reflected ray
C. normal line
D. focal point
E. image axis
5. [DOK 1] Which phenomenon demonstrates refraction?
A. echo
B. shadow
C. mirror image
D. straw bent in water
E. warming shirt
6. [DOK 2] A light ray strikes a mirror with angle of incidence $35^\circ$. What is the reflected angle?
A. 0°
B. 35°
C. 55°
D. 70°
E. 145°
7. [DOK 2] Light travels through a material at $2.0\times10^8\,m/s$. What is the index of refraction?
A. 0.67
B. 1.0
C. 1.5
D. 2.0
E. 6.0
8. [DOK 2] A ray enters glass from air and slows down. What happens?
A. bends away from normal
B. bends toward normal
C. reflects only
D. stops
E. becomes sound
9. [DOK 2] A plane mirror is 2 m in front of a student. Where does the image appear?
A. 1 m behind
B. 2 m behind
C. 4 m behind
D. on surface
E. no image
10. [DOK 2] An image is 6 cm tall while object is 3 cm tall. What is magnification?
A. 0.5
B. 1
C. 2
D. 3
E. 6
11. [DOK 2] A converging lens forms an image projected on a screen. The image is:
A. virtual
B. reflected only
C. real
D. impossible
E. opaque
12. [DOK 3] A student says 'Mirrors reverse left and right.' Which response best corrects this?
A. mirrors reverse up/down
B. mirrors reverse front/back relative to observer
C. mirrors rotate images
D. mirrors absorb motion
E. mirrors create no image
13. [DOK 3] A straw appears bent in water because:
A. water bends straw
B. refraction changes apparent location
C. reflection destroys image
D. light stops
E. straw emits light
14. [DOK 3] A rainbow forms mainly because:
A. reflection only
B. dispersion separates colors
C. sky paints colors
D. sound interferes
E. cloud mirrors
15. [DOK 3] A nearsighted eye focuses light in front of the retina. Corrective lenses help because:
A. stop reflection
B. change how rays bend
C. create new light
D. remove retina
E. reduce wavelength
16. [DOK 4] A company claims a coating eliminates all glare from every viewing angle. Best evaluation?
A. correct always
B. reflection fully removed
C. incorrect because glare depends on angle/material/wavelength
D. glare only from mirrors
E. correct for black surfaces
Free Response — Questions 17–20
Show work for full credit. Clearly identify final answer(s).
17. Light travels through a material at $2.0\times10^8\,m/s$. Calculate the index of refraction and explain what the value means physically.
18. A light ray enters glass from air at an angle. Explain whether it bends toward or away from the normal and why.
19. A student says 'A mirror flips everything left-to-right.' Critique the statement using virtual images and observer orientation.
20. A website claims optical fibers work because mirrors bounce light perfectly forever. Critique using total internal reflection.
